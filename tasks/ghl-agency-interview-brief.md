# GHL Agency Interview Brief — Kel (180-client agency owner)
*Stress-test the core idea before building more.*

---

## What I'm really testing

At 180 clients, GHL runs out of room. No way to see all clients at once. No safe way to run AI everywhere. No clean view of what each client costs or earns.

We're not a better GHL. We sit on top of it.

If Kel feels that pain, we have a design partner. If he doesn't, we need to rethink.

---

## How to open

"So Kel, as you know I come from a software development background. For the last six months I've been building an automation operating system that sits on top of all the tools a business runs on -- GoHighLevel, Gmail, Calendar, Slack, and others -- and deploys a full team of AI agents that run your business for you. I'm trying to work out the best initial use case, and my thinking is GHL agencies at your scale are probably feeling this more than anyone.

Here's what I'm building toward. You wake up Monday, open your laptop, and instead of logging into 180 accounts you've got one screen. Every client has a health score. Anything that needs attention is already flagged. Your agents ran overnight across every client -- follow-ups, reporting, content, support -- and nothing went out without your approval. There's a queue of actions your agents are about to take on behalf of your clients, work they'll do that you get paid for. You can see exactly what you made before the day even starts.

And here's the bigger shift: the same AI workforce that runs your agency is something you can resell to every client. What costs you a few hundred a month becomes a service you charge thousands for.

That's what I'm building. I just want to make sure I'm building it for the right person. Mind if I ask you a few questions?"

---

## Four things GHL can't do, no matter what they ship

1. See all clients at once, with health scores and alerts
2. Approval gates on anything AI sends to a client
3. Agents running overnight, logging everything, ready to resell
4. Set it up once, runs across every client

---

## Four questions

**1. How do you spot trouble on a Monday morning?**
*"Walk me through it. You've got 180 clients. What do you open first?"*
Listen for: manual logins, gut feel, "my team tells me," "I hear about it when the client complains." Confirms whether the picture you just painted lands as a real contrast.

**2. Has AI ever gone wrong for a client?**
*"Are you running AI for clients right now? Has it ever said something wrong or off-brand? How did you find out?"*
Listen for: a real story with a wince. No story means they're not running AI at scale or not catching failures. Either is a problem.

**3. What's the one thing you'd do across all 180 clients if you could?**
*"If you could do one thing across every client at once, without setting it up 180 times, what would it be?"*
Listen for anything that maps to overnight automation or repetitive work. This tells you what the agents should actually be doing.

**4. What would this be worth?**
*"Based on what I've described -- one screen, agents running overnight, an approval queue, and a workforce you can resell -- what's that worth per month? What would you need to see to trust it?"*
Listen for a real number. "A few hundred" is nice-to-have. "A thousand or two" is real pain. "Whatever it costs if it works" means build them a demo this week.

---

## Green flags
Real AI incident story / can't see across clients easily / lights up at the Monday morning picture / four-figure number without flinching / happy to give 30 minutes a week

## Red flags
"GHL AI works fine" / loves their current dashboards / no incident story / dodging the conversation / won't commit time

---

## The line I want to hear

*"We can't see across all our clients, and every time we turn AI up it breaks something so we turn it back down."*

If I hear that, we have a wedge. If I don't, the GHL path needs a rethink.
