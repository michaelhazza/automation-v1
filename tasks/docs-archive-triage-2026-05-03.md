## Why the heuristics fail here

The `docs/` directory is tightly cross-linked:
- Specs reference each other (a brief points at its spec, the spec points back at the brief, both reference the contracts they touch).
- Briefs persist past their spec because they carry the rationale.
- "Superseded" appears in body text frequently because it's a domain term (artefact lifecycle uses it).

Reliable archive requires an **explicit retirement convention at the top of each spec** — one we don't have today. That gap was the prompt for the new Section 11 in `docs/spec-authoring-checklist.md` (added 2026-05-03):

```markdown
**Status:** draft | reviewing | accepted | shipped | superseded by <path-or-ADR>
```

Once specs from 2026-05-03 forward carry this header, a future archive sweep becomes a one-line grep against the header line — no false positives from body text.

New specs (per the updated checklist) carry `Status:` headers. Run an archive sweep in 6 months on the new specs only. Old specs stay in `docs/` indefinitely.

Operator (or `audit-runner` in a focused mode) reads every spec, classifies, and writes the header. ~3-4 hours of focused work. Produces a clean `docs/` for the first time.
