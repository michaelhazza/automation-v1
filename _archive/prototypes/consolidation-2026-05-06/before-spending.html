Problems with current state

  • 1
    Two pages for related data. Cap configuration lives at /admin/spending-budgets and usage history lives at /admin/subaccounts/:id/spend-ledger. An admin who notices an over-cap situation must navigate between two unrelated routes to understand and act.
  • 2
    No org-wide ledger view. The Spend Ledger is strictly per-subaccount. There is no consolidated view across all 4 subaccounts. Comparing total spend requires visiting each subaccount individually and summing manually.
  • 3
    Cap fields have no spend context. The Spending Budgets page shows cap values in cents (e.g. "150000") with no current-spend alongside them, no progress bar, no pace indicator, and no colour signal for accounts approaching their cap. An admin cannot tell at a glance which subaccounts are at risk.
  • 4
    Caps stored and displayed in cents. dailyCostLimitCents and monthlyCostLimitCents are shown raw. An admin editing a cap must mentally convert dollars to cents and back. The new design converts at the UI layer.
  • 5
    Ledger group-by is limited to "Day" only. No group by Agent, Source type, or Subaccount. An admin wanting to find which agent is driving up spend must scan the flat list manually.
  • 6
    No row drill-down. Clicking a ledger row does nothing. Provider, model, token counts, cache discount, and run trace link are all unavailable from the ledger surface.
