Reverse promotion (§6.10)
If the user later flips back to Explore from Execute, the counter resets to 0 and the auto-prompt stays silent until they rebuild trust.
Click-through trap: defences
  • Requires 5 consecutive approvals (no rejections reset is optional — spec §12).
  • Trust receipts shown — user sees what they're actually auto-approving.
  • "Not yet" is the left-positioned, non-accent button (not a dark pattern).
  • Escape hatch line in footer reminds them they can revert.
Open question (spec §6.10): does a single button click feel earned after 5 Explore runs, or should we require a short typed confirmation (e.g. typing "Execute")? Reviewer to call.
